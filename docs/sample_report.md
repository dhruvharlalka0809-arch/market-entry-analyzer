# Sample Output: Market Entry Report

This is a representative output format for the Market Entry Strategy Analyzer. It is a sample for portfolio review and should not be treated as live investment advice.

## 1. Executive Summary

- Verdict: **Enter with conditions**
- Confidence: Medium
- The recommendation would change if customer acquisition costs exceed the modeled payback threshold or if regulatory barriers delay launch beyond 12 months.

## 2. Market Overview

The target market should be sized from a broad sourced market estimate, narrowed by geography, buyer segment, and realistic adoption assumptions. If the search pass cannot find reliable current data, the report must state that directly rather than inventing a number.

Expected output:

- broad market estimate
- narrowing logic
- final serviceable market estimate
- visible source citations

## 3. Competitive Landscape

The report identifies 3-5 relevant competitors and summarizes their positioning, scale, pricing, or product focus using source-backed facts.

Expected output:

- direct competitors
- adjacent substitutes
- competitive intensity
- source-backed differentiators

## 4. Risks & Entry Barriers

Risks are ranked by severity and tied to evidence where possible.

Common risk categories:

- regulation
- incumbent competition
- distribution access
- payment behavior
- localization requirements
- switching costs

## 5. Decision Thresholds

The output must include at least one quantified decision rule.

Example:

> This recommendation holds if gross margin remains above 35% and CAC payback stays below 12 months. It reverses if payback exceeds 18 months or launch compliance costs exceed the base case by more than 25%.

## 6. Recommendation Detail

The final section explains why the verdict is justified and must not contradict the Executive Summary.

Expected output:

- why the company should enter, avoid, or enter with conditions
- what assumptions matter most
- what evidence should be validated next
- recommended next steps

## How This Was Produced

The tool appends a transparency footer showing:

- number of sources found
- number of claims checked
- whether unsupported claims were flagged
- whether verdict and recommendation were consistent
- whether decision thresholds were present
